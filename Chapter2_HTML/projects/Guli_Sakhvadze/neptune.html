Neptune

Dark, cold and whipped by supersonic winds, ice giant Neptune is the eighth and most distant planet
in our solar system.


More than 30 times as far from the Sun as Earth, Neptune is the only planet in our solar system not visible to the naked eye
and the first predicted by mathematics before its discovery. In 2011 Neptune completed its first 165-year orbit since its
discovery in 1846.


In the Solar System Neptune is:

  • The fourth-largest planet by diameter;
  • The third-most-massive planet, and the densest giant planet;
  • The eighth and farthest-known Solar planet from the Sun.
